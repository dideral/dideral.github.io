How to find your best targeted Therapy?



Each person has their own finger print. The genetic identity is just as individual.

Therefore it is not surprising that cancer cells also differ from each other, even for the same types of cancer.

How to Find Your Best Targeted Therapy

Modern anticancer therapies are based on the knowledge about the molecular events which cause a cell to become a tumor cell. In contrast to common cures such as chemo- or radiotherapy research worldwide focuses on targeted therapy. New active agents are being developed, which hit the Achilles’ heel of a tumor.

For instance, ONCOMPASS™ is a complex diagnostic and information system. It is able to locate gene mutations, which are responsible for the formation of tumors. This is the cornerstone for a targeted anticancer therapy.

Targeted therapy intervenes in processes which specifically cause cancer to develop. CEIC assists your search for a personalized, targeted therapy.

Background: The most frequent types of cancer such as lung-, colorectal- and breast cancer originate in the epithelium. About 138 driver genes and more than one million possible mutations are responsible for that. If the individual mutation profile and genetic defects of a patient are known, it is possible to specifically search for effective drugs. A lot of new drugs are launched worldwide, hundreds are still being developed. Which tumor is sensible to which drug? In which cases do resistances to drugs arise? ONCOMPASS™ keeps an overview of all of these developments through a continuously updated data base, which also includes information about current clinical studies.


ONCOMPASS™: How does the test work?

ONCOMPASS™ comprises three steps:


  • There is a multitude of cancer genes and 1 to 8 are mutated in every tumor. To find out, which genes are responsible for the growth of your tumor, the DNA from your tissue sample is analyzed. This molecular diagnostic examination detects the possible genetic defects of more than half of the genes responsible for tumor development.


  • After the mutated genes are uncovered, ONCOMPASS™ determines the best targeted therapies worldwide, which are available today. For that the effect of more than 200 targeted drugs are compared with your cancer genes. The result contains a list of potentially effective and also ineffective drugs.


  • With the help of the ONCOMPASS™ service, your treating physician is able to integrate targeted therapies in your own treatment regimen. While it is possible that chemotherapy, radiotherapy and surgery are your best treatment options right now, ONCOMPASS™ provides information about when and where targeted therapies are accessible. A growing network of partner physicians in more than ten countries assists you during the search for the best available treatment for you.
